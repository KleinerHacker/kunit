enum KVoltageUnit : KUnit, Enum<KVoltageUnit>

Enumerates concrete units of voltage (electric potential difference, a constructed quantity: mass¹ · distance² · time⁻³ · current⁻¹, i.e. kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻¹). baseValue is the factor to convert into the group's base unit (BASE, volt): 1 unit = baseValue * V.

KVoltageUnitInstance
Link copied to clipboard
class KVoltageUnitInstance : KUnitInstance<KVoltageUnitInstance> , KUnitMeasurable

Wraps a KMixedUnitInstance representing a voltage, i.e. exactly four terms in the canonical normal form mass¹ · distance² · time⁻³ · current⁻¹ (kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻¹). The value is always normalized internally to volts (KVoltageUnit.BASE), regardless of which KVoltageUnit, SI org.pcsoft.framework.kunit.KUnitPrefix, or mass/length/time/current combination it was constructed from.

toVoltage

fun KMixedUnitInstance.toVoltage(): KVoltageUnitInstance

Converts this mixed unit to a "pure" voltage value, as long as it matches the canonical voltage normal form: exactly one KMassUnit term at exponent +1, one KDistanceUnit term at exponent +2, one KTimeUnit term at exponent -3 and one KElectricCurrentUnit term at exponent -1 (order independent). The terms are normalized over their org.pcsoft.framework.kunit.KUnit.baseValues, so the resulting voltage is expressed in volts regardless of which concrete mass/length/time/current units the terms were tagged with.
